## What is the Pomodoro Technique?
Developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s, the Pomodoro Technique breaks tasks into manageable intervals, typically 25 minutes long, separated by short breaks. Named after the Italian word for "tomato," Cirillo initially used a tomato-shaped kitchen timer to track work intervals.

## How Does it Help Maintain Focus?
In an age of digital distractions, the Pomodoro Technique provides a structured approach to combatting this issue. Breaking work into focused intervals prevents burnout and decision fatigue while promoting sustained concentration.

## How to Use it?
1. __Choose__ a Task: Select a task to work on, whether it's writing a report or studying for an exam.
2. __Set the Timer__: Set a Pomodoro Timer for 25 minutes and focus solely on the task at hand.
3. __Work Intensely__: Dive into your work with full concentration until the timer rings.
4. __Take a Short Break__: After each interval, take a 5-minute break to refresh your mind.
5. __Repeat the Process__: Complete four intervals, then take a longer break of 15-30 minutes.
